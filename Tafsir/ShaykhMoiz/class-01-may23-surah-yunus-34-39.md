*A detailed summary of the Tafsir lesson covering the continuation of Surah Yunus -- Allah's exclusive power to create, the centrality of hidayat, the danger of following assumptions and feelings over revelation, the inimitable nature of the Quran, why people reject what they don't understand, and the different responses of hearts to the Quran*

## Recap: Why Do People Turn Away from Allah?

In the previous lesson, Allah asked a series of questions: Who provides rizq? Who controls all the affairs of your life? Who governs every single thing? Even the disbelievers admitted that it is Allah who provides everything, controls everything, governs everything.

The question then became: if the truth is so clear, why do people still turn away from Allah? The answer: **when a person continues to sin** -- repeatedly, persistently -- there comes a stage where their **iman is in danger**. The heart no longer feels anything. The heart becomes dead.

That is why the scholars say: **be very careful about taking that first step in the direction of sin.** If a person takes that step and doesn't make tawbah, the first sin leads to the next, the next leads to the third, and as a person keeps persisting, they reach a stage where their iman becomes endangered. So one should try to stay away from that very first sin.

## Verse 34: Who Creates and Then Recreates?

> **قُلْ هَلْ مِنْ شُرَكَائِكُمْ مَنْ يَبْدَأُ الْخَلْقَ ثُمَّ يُعِيدُهُ**

*"Say: Is there anyone from your associates who originates creation and then brings it back again?"*

This is a challenge. Allah is telling the Prophet (peace be upon him) to challenge the disbelievers: can whatever you're worshiping create life? And when life comes to an end, can they resurrect? Can they control existence itself?

One of the names of Allah is **Al-Khaliq** -- the Creator. Creation -- to create something from nothing -- is one of the ultimate signs of Allah. Something only Allah can do.

### Relevance to Us Today

Maybe we don't worship statues or stones or the sun and moon. But our **idols are different**. The idols that we have are not of stone and clay -- they are idols **within our hearts**: the idols of money, name, fame, status, image.

### Allah's Response

> **قُلِ اللهُ يَبْدَأُ الْخَلْقَ ثُمَّ يُعِيدُهُ فَأَنَّى تُؤْفَكُونَ**

*"Say: Allah originates creation and then brings it back. So where are you being deluded?"*

### Two Important Words: Khalq and Ibda'

There is a subtle difference:

- **خَلْقٌ (Khalq)** -- to create something **from something**. You take atoms, particles, molecules, organize them, and create something out of existing matter. Creating from a plan, from basic matter.
- **إِبْدَاءٌ (Ibda')** -- to create something **from nothing**. Absolute origination.

Allah has **both** these attributes. He is Khaliq -- creates from existing things. And He also creates from absolute nothing. Only Allah created you, only He sustains you, and only He will resurrect you. So why attach your heart to other things? Why depend on creation when the Creator controls everything?

## Verse 35: Who Guides to the Truth?

> **قُلْ هَلْ مِنْ شُرَكَائِكُمْ مَنْ يَهْدِي إِلَى الْحَقِّ**

*"Say: Is there anyone from your associates who guides to the truth?"*

There is a **shift** here -- from physical creation to spiritual guidance. Physical life is not enough. We have our physical body, but we also have our spiritual self, our **ruh**. We don't just need physical sustenance -- we need **spiritual direction**. We need the nourishment of the heart, and that is **hidayat**.

### Hidayat is Greater Than Rizq

One of the famous mufassireen (commentators of the Quran) said that **guidance is even greater than provision (rizq)**. How do we know this? Because the very first du'a that Allah teaches us in the Quran -- the du'a that the Prophet (peace be upon him) made, every Sahabi made, and that we will need for our **entire** life -- is the du'a for hidayat:

> **اِهْدِنَا الصِّرَاطَ الْمُسْتَقِيمَ**

*"Guide us to the straight path."* (Surah al-Fatiha 1:6)

We can never reach a stage in our journey where we can say: "I don't need this du'a anymore. I already have hidayat." This is the very first du'a in the Quran. The very first help that we need. In Surah al-Fatiha, when we declare that we worship Allah alone and seek help from Him alone, Allah is teaching us: the very first help you need from Me is **hidayat**. Ask it from Me.

### The Emptiness Without Hidayat

Many people have money, every comfort, entertainment, recreation, vacations -- all the luxuries of life. And yet they feel an **emptiness** within their heart. An anxiety, a spiritual vacuum, a hollowness. This is because hidayat is missing. You can have everything materially and yet be completely hollow and empty from within. The heart has different needs from the body. The heart needs hidayat, nourishment, connection with Allah.

### Multiple Layers of Hidayat

The Shaykh explained that hidayat has multiple layers:

1. Being **guided to** the straight path
2. Being guided on **how to walk** on the straight path
3. Guidance to **keep walking** on the straight path
4. Guidance to **remain steadfast** on it
5. Guidance to **reach the destination**

From start to finish, we need hidayat so that we remain connected to the straight path, keep walking on it, and eventually die in a state where Allah is pleased with us.

### Hidayat is a Gift from Allah

> **قُلِ اللهُ يَهْدِي لِلْحَقِّ ۗ أَفَمَنْ يَهْدِي إِلَى الْحَقِّ أَحَقُّ أَنْ يُتَّبَعَ أَمَّنْ لَا يَهِدِّي إِلَّا أَنْ يُهْدَىٰ**

*"Say: Allah guides to the truth. Is then He who guides to the truth more worthy of being followed, or he who has no guide at all unless he himself is guided?"*

Guidance will **not** come through intelligence alone. Not through education, degrees, talent. There are many brilliant philosophers, intellectuals, scientists who are spiritually empty, spiritually confused, distant from Allah, distant from Islam. **Hidayat is a gift from Allah.** He gives it to whomsoever He wishes. It's a light He places in the heart.

### Present Yourself for Hidayat

One should try to place themselves in gatherings, locations, times where they present themselves to Allah asking for guidance. The Shaykh mentioned the **first ten days of Dhul Hijjah** (the blessed days they were in at the time) -- days where every good deed is more beloved to Allah than at any other time of the year.

### Stories of Radical Transformation

This is an **ummah of change**. People change overnight:

- A person who spent their whole life in sin -- one night, Allah's gaze of hidayat falls upon them, their heart flips, and the next day they become a wali of Allah
- **Drunkards** have become friends and lovers of Allah
- **Umar** (may Allah be pleased with him) -- he left his house with a **naked sword**, intending to martyr the Prophet (peace be upon him). Then Allah's gaze of hidayat, of love, of rahma, of acceptance fell upon him. Allah chose him for the rank of **Sahabi**, then **Amir al-Mu'mineen**, **Khalifat al-Rasul**. The Prophet (peace be upon him) used to say that when Umar walks one path, Shaytan takes another pathway.

All of us just need **that one gaze of Allah's rahma**. One gaze is sufficient. But we should keep trying, keep making effort, keep presenting ourselves before Allah.

## Verse 36: Most People Follow Nothing But Assumptions

> **وَمَا يَتَّبِعُ أَكْثَرُهُمْ إِلَّا ظَنًّا**

*"Most of them follow nothing but assumptions (zan)."*

This is an extremely relevant and pertinent ayah. Most people do not follow certainty -- they follow **zan** (conjecture, guesswork, assumptions). They are not following haqq. They're following their assumptions, some ism, some philosophy -- liberalism, secular humanism -- these intellectual currents that **keep changing** every hundred, two hundred, three hundred years. The fact that they keep changing is itself a sign that they are just assumptions, not truth.

### The Crisis of "Just Follow Your Feelings"

In our generation, this has become even more relevant with social media and the culture of following feelings:

- "There is no objective truth"
- "There is no absolute truth"
- "Just follow your feelings"
- "Everyone has their own truth"
- "Why do you do this?" -- "Because I feel this is right"
- "Why should I do this?" -- "Because everyone else is doing it"

The Shaykh called this a **tragedy of our times** -- that we have gone from revelation, knowledge, evidence, objectivity, truth -- down to the level of "just do what you feel like doing." This is an **animalistic level**. Animals follow their feelings and passions. Where is knowledge? Where is evidence? Where is revelation?

As a result, we see absurdities -- people saying things like "I feel I'm a dog, I feel I'm a cat." This is exactly what Allah warned about in the Quran:

> **أَفَرَأَيْتَ مَنِ اتَّخَذَ إِلٰهَهُ هَوَاهُ**

*"Have you seen the one who has taken his desires as his god?"*

They are worshiping their whims, fancies, caprices, passions. Allah says these are the misguided ones.

### Feelings Are Real But Not Always True

People can have feelings, but feelings are not always truth. Many life choices today are built on feelings, trends, emotions, social pressure. Our morality -- what is good, what is bad -- has been reduced to what culture and social media dictate. Concepts of beauty, ugliness, right, wrong -- all shifting with every season and every year.

### Assumptions Have No Significance Before Truth

> **إِنَّ الظَّنَّ لَا يُغْنِي مِنَ الْحَقِّ شَيْئًا ۚ إِنَّ اللهَ عَلِيمٌ بِمَا يَفْعَلُونَ**

*"Indeed, assumption avails nothing against the truth. Indeed, Allah is fully knowing of what they do."*

These shifting, subjective feelings and assumptions have **no significance** compared to the objective truth laid down by Allah in revelation (**Wahi**). And we can fool people -- our parents, our spouse, our children, society -- sometimes we even fool ourselves, rationalizing and justifying to our nafs. But **we can never fool Allah**. A sin is a sin, no matter how much we try to redefine it.

### Have Trust in the Islamic Tradition

The Shaykh shared from personal experience, having studied at one of the top institutions for philosophy of religion in the United States (Indiana). He said that **every single objection** raised against Islam from the other side -- when you go and study the Islamic tradition, you will find answers given by our own scholars. In many cases, **multiple answers** have been given. The scholars anticipated many of those questions.

One should not be scared or terrified. The Islamic scholarly tradition is over fourteen hundred years old, intellectually very robust and spiritually very grounded. There is not a single objection or question that doesn't have an answer within our tradition.

## Verse 37: The Quran Could Never Be Fabricated

> **وَمَا كَانَ هٰذَا الْقُرْآنُ أَنْ يُفْتَرَىٰ مِنْ دُونِ اللهِ وَلٰكِنْ تَصْدِيقَ الَّذِي بَيْنَ يَدَيْهِ وَتَفْصِيلَ الْكِتَابِ لَا رَيْبَ فِيهِ مِنْ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ**

*"This Quran is not such as could have been fabricated by anyone other than Allah. Rather, it is a confirmation of what came before it, and a detailed exposition of the Scripture -- in which there is no doubt -- from the Lord of the worlds."*

### The Quran is Not Man-Made

The Quran is not poetry, not philosophy, not feelings, not emotional speech. It is **divine speech** -- the kalam of Allah. It is **suprarational** -- it transcends rationality. It's perfect. It cannot be a human creation because humans are imperfect. The Quran is:

- **Perfect in its language**
- **Perfect in its guidance**
- **Perfect in the wisdom it imparts**

This perfection proves that its source is not human -- it is divine.

### The Transformative Power of the Quran

The Quran transformed people who were living in **Jahiliyyah** -- burying their daughters alive, fighting over petty things for decades. It made them into **world leaders**. It created a whole civilization out of the Arabian desert -- a wasteland that even the two world superpowers (Persia and Rome) couldn't care less about. And this Quran is **still transforming** people today, all across the world.

### A Powerful Story from a US Prison

The Shaykh shared a story: A da'ee working in a US prison gave da'wah to an inmate who accepted Islam. When asked what changed, the man said:

> "Before Islam, I was a murderer. I killed people just for the thrill of it. I would dance around the body, relishing the sight. It wasn't personal, no revenge, no money -- I just loved it. Now, with the Quran, with iman, when the guards let us out to walk in the courtyard, **I am so careful lifting my feet lest an ant comes beneath and I trample over it.**"

This is the transformation of the Quran. A murderer who killed for thrill -- now afraid of stepping on an ant.

### The Quran Confirms Previous Revelation

The Quran is the **only scripture** that confirms all previous scriptures from Allah -- the Torah revealed to Musa (peace be upon him), the Zabur revealed to Dawud (peace be upon him), the Injil. Believing in the Quran means believing in all previous revelations. The core message has always been the same: worship Allah alone, purify your heart, don't give in to desires, prepare for the akhirah. Only the **Sharia** (the specific laws and practices) changed from prophet to prophet.

The Quran is also a **tafsil** (exposition) -- giving detailed explanation. And there is **no doubt** in it. The doubt is not in the Quran -- the doubt might be in the heart of the person reading it. The Quran itself is absolutely doubt-free.

## Verse 38: The Challenge of the Quran

> **أَمْ يَقُولُونَ افْتَرَاهُ ۖ قُلْ فَأْتُوا بِسُورَةٍ مِثْلِهِ وَادْعُوا مَنِ اسْتَطَعْتُمْ مِنْ دُونِ اللهِ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ صَادِقِينَ**

*"Or do they say he fabricated it? Say: Then bring one surah like it, and call upon whomsoever you can besides Allah, if you are truthful."*

This is an **open challenge** -- to every era, every time period, past, present, and future. Bring just **one surah** like the Quran. Call whoever you want to help you. The Arab poets, the greatest masters of eloquence and **balagha** -- they were challenged and found themselves to be no match. Today, professors, philosophers, literary critics -- the Quran challenges them all.

The Quran continues to:
- **Heal hearts**
- **Transform lives**
- **Remain unmatched**

No other book does this. Other books give temporary entertainment, brief inspiration. But a book that fundamentally transforms a person's soul, character, and heart -- and has been doing so for fourteen hundred years and will continue till the end of time -- there is only one: the **Kalam of Allah, the Quran**.

## Verse 39: People Reject What They Don't Understand

> **بَلْ كَذَّبُوا بِمَا لَمْ يُحِيطُوا بِعِلْمِهِ وَلَمَّا يَأْتِهِمْ تَأْوِيلُهُ**

*"Rather, they denied that which they could not encompass in knowledge, while its interpretation has not yet come to them."*

This is an extremely deep ayah. People **reject what they don't understand**. This is part of human nature -- what you cannot fully grasp, you start rejecting. Allah is saying: don't do this. This is **ignorance combined with arrogance**.

### Examples of Rejection Without Understanding

- People reject **Islamic rulings** without fully studying them -- just based on hearsay
- People reject **spiritual practices** in Islam -- calling certain forms of dhikr bid'ah without truly studying the issue
- People belittle **Sunan** -- "Oh, it's just Sunnah, it's not fard" -- without understanding the true makam (rank) of the Sunnah
- People say **hijab doesn't make sense** and leave it
- People question: "Why pray five times? Allah doesn't need my prayer"
- People say **religion is too restrictive** and become lazy about their deen

All of this -- without fully understanding, without study, without diving deep into what the scholars have written.

### The Importance of 'Ilm (Knowledge) as a First Step

The scholars have written **books upon books** explaining the reason, rationale, and wisdom behind every ruling. The Shaykh emphasized: **'ilm is crucial as a first step.** At least try to understand what the deen is before making a choice. If after studying you make an informed decision, that's one thing. But to reject without studying -- because of something someone said on social media -- that is what Allah is warning against.

Many people reject Islam just because of **emotions**, because they don't like something. Even practicing Muslims do this -- they selectively do amal on parts of the Quran that are within their comfort zone and avoid the rest. They avoid lectures where they know they'll be told to do things they don't want to do. Why? Because the **nafs wants comfort**, and we have made ourselves so comfortable that we're not willing to make sacrifice for the sake of Allah.

## Verse 39 (continued): Hearts Respond Differently to the Quran

> **وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ يُؤْمِنُ بِهِ وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ لَا يُؤْمِنُ بِهِ**

*"Among them are those who believe in it, and among them are those who do not believe in it."*

The same Quran is heard by everyone. But hearts respond differently. One person hears an ayah and **cries** -- tears fall, the heart trembles, they make tawbah and seek forgiveness. Another person hears the **same ayah** and feels nothing. They remain unchanged.

The issue is not the Quran -- the Quran's power, meaning, and impact are the same. The issue is **our hearts**. The hearts are different -- that's why the responses are different.

### The Pure Heart vs. the Hardened Heart

- A **pure, purified heart** -- soft, cleansed of sin, where tazkiyah has been done -- the Quran will melt it. The ayat will sink deep and transform it. Tears will flow.
- A **hardened heart** -- rigid, tainted with sin, with no effort made to purify it -- it becomes dark and resistant. It's listening to the Quran but not impacted. It has become, as the Shaykh put it, **"Quran-resistant"** -- like steel-resistant, fire-resistant, water-resistant. The heart is hearing the Quran but not changing. Why? Because it is stained with sins that have hardened it.

### Tazkiyah Before Ta'lim

That is why there is so much importance placed on **purifying the heart**. When Allah described the four functions of the Prophet (peace be upon him), the ordering is significant. Allah placed **tazkiyah** (purification of the heart) **first**, and **ta'lim** (teaching the Quran) **after**. First purify the hearts, then teach the Quran. Once the hearts are purified, they become receptive to receiving the nur and the barakah of the Quran. Then the hearts will be transformed when they listen to the Quran.
