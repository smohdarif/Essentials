## The Hadith: The Man Who Prayed Incorrectly (Hadith 790)

### Translation of the Hadith

A person entered the masjid. The Prophet (peace be upon him) was sitting in a **corner** of the masjid (not in the middle, but to one side). That person went and prayed. After finishing, he came to the Prophet (peace be upon him) and said salam. The Prophet (peace be upon him) replied to his salam, then said:

> **"Go back and pray, because you have not prayed."**

The person went back and prayed again. He came back, did salam, and the Prophet (peace be upon him) again said:

> **"Go back and pray, because you have not prayed."**

This happened **two times** in full detail. The narrator (Rawi) then summarized and said: "The third time or the fourth time" -- meaning the narrator did not repeat the full scenario again but indicated it happened again.

Finally, the Sahabi said:

> **"Allimni ya Rasulallah"** -- "O Messenger of Allah, teach me!"

### What the Prophet (peace be upon him) Taught Him

1. **Complete the wudu (ablution) fully** -- this is the very first step before prayer.
2. **Face the Qibla.**
3. **Say the Takbir** (Allahu Akbar).
4. **Read whatever is easy for you from the Quran** -- meaning whatever you have memorized that you can read easily (Surah al-Fatiha and a surah).
5. **Do Ruku (bow) until you are at ease while bowing** -- bow completely and properly.
6. **Rise until you are standing upright** -- this standing after ruku is called **Qawmah**.
7. **Do Sajdah (prostrate) until you are at ease while prostrating.**
8. **Rise until you are at ease while sitting** -- this sitting between two sajdahs is called **Jalsa**.
9. **Do the second Sajdah until you are at ease while prostrating.**
10. **Rise until you are at ease while sitting.**
11. **Then rise until you are standing upright** -- stand for the second rak'ah.

### Important Note About the Scope of This Hadith

The teacher clarified that from the beginning of this hadith's instructions to the end, it is only describing **one rak'ah** -- not the entire prayer.

## Discussion: Jalsa al-Istirahah (The Brief Sitting Before Standing)

This is a key point of difference that emerged from the hadith:

- In one narration (wording), after the second sajdah of the first rak'ah, it says: **sit** (at ease while sitting) **before standing** for the second rak'ah. This brief sitting is called **Jalsa al-Istirahah** -- literally "a sitting of rest," like taking a small break before standing up.

- In **another narration/wording**, it says: **stand up directly** -- do not sit before standing for the second rak'ah.

### Imam Bukhari's Position

- Imam Bukhari indicated (made isharah) by bringing specific wordings that **Jalsa al-Istirahah is not required** in his view. He considered it **not necessary** (mansookh/not needed).
- Hafiz Ibn Hajar confirmed this is what Imam Bukhari meant.

### Difference Between Madhabs

- **Shafi'i madhab:** Jalsa al-Istirahah is practiced -- before standing for the second rak'ah, you sit briefly for a moment.
- **Hanafi madhab:** No Jalsa al-Istirahah -- from the second sajdah, you go **straight to standing** for the second rak'ah without sitting.

## Key Terminology Explained

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Tatma'innah** | Doing each action of prayer properly, completely, and with ease |
| **Ta'dil al-Arkan** | Performing all the postures/pillars of prayer properly and with composure |
| **Qawmah** | Standing upright after Ruku, before going to Sajdah |
| **Jalsa** | Sitting between the two Sajdahs |
| **Qa'dah Ula** | The first sitting for Tashahud (after 2 rak'ahs) |
| **Qa'dah Thaniya** | The second/final sitting for Tashahud (after 4th rak'ah) |
| **Jalsa al-Istirahah** | A brief sitting before standing for the next rak'ah (Shafi'i practice) |

## The Big Scholarly Debate: Is Ta'dil al-Arkan Fard or Wajib?

This is the central fiqhi discussion of this hadith.

### Position of Imam Shafi'i and the Three Imams (A'immah Thalathah)

- **Ta'dil al-Arkan is Fard (compulsory).** If someone does not do it, their prayer is **not accepted/invalid**.
- **Their proof:** This very hadith -- the Prophet (peace be upon him) said "Go back and pray, because you have not prayed." This is like declining/rejecting his prayer entirely.

### Position of Imam Abu Hanifah / Imam Muhammad (Hanafi Madhab)

- **Ta'dil al-Arkan is Wajib**, not Fard. If someone misses it, the prayer is deficient but not automatically void.

### The Hanafi Proofs

**Proof 1 -- From the Quran:**

> **وَارْكَعُوا وَاسْجُدُوا**

"And bow down and prostrate." (The command is to do ruku and sujood.) The Hanafis say: this command is fulfilled even if the ruku and sujood are brief. The Quran commands ruku and sujood -- even a short bowing and prostration fulfills the Quranic command. So ta'dil al-arkan (doing it at length with ease) is an addition beyond the Quranic command.

**Proof 2 -- Another Hadith (about stealing in prayer):**

The Prophet (peace be upon him) said that the **worst person (aswa' al-nas)** is the one who **steals in prayer**. The Sahaba asked: "How can someone steal in prayer?" The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: **"The one who does not complete his ruku and sujood."**

The Hanafis point out: In this hadith, the Prophet (peace be upon him) called such a person the **worst** but did **not** say his prayer is invalid/not accepted. This supports ta'dil al-arkan being wajib (its absence makes the prayer severely deficient) rather than fard (whose absence would make the prayer void).

**Proof 3 -- The Principle of Khabar Wahid:**

The hadith of the man who prayed badly is a **Khabar Wahid** (narrated through a single Sahabi). According to Hanafi usul, a Khabar Wahid **cannot add to the Quran** (Ziyadah ala al-Kitab). Since the Quran only commands ruku and sujood (without specifying tatma'innah), and the Quran is stronger than a single-narrator hadith, we follow the Quran first. When the hadith and Quran seem to differ in their hukm, the Quran takes precedence.

## Why Did the Prophet (peace be upon him) Not Teach Him the First Time?

Three answers were given:

**Answer 1:** The Sahabi **did not ask** the first time. He didn't say "teach me." So the Prophet (peace be upon him) simply told him to go back and pray. Only when the Sahabi finally asked "Allimni ya Rasulallah" (teach me) did the Prophet (peace be upon him) give the detailed instructions.

**Answer 2:** The Prophet (peace be upon him) thought perhaps the Sahabi was making a **mistake or had forgotten**. By sending him back, the Prophet (peace be upon him) hoped the Sahabi might **remember on his own** how to pray properly. But since the Sahabi kept repeating the same error, the Prophet (peace be upon him) then taught him directly.

**Answer 3 (from some Hanafi scholars -- Ba'd Hanafiyyah):**

- If a person misses Ta'dil al-Arkan (since it is wajib in the Hanafi madhab), then **Sajdah Sahw becomes obligatory** on that person.
- Since this Sahabi neither did Ta'dil al-Arkan **nor** did Sajdah Sahw, the Prophet (peace be upon him) said "you have not prayed."
- The reasoning: leaving a wajib requires Sajdah Sahw to compensate. If you leave a wajib AND also don't do Sajdah Sahw, then the prayer is invalidated.
- This is the position mentioned by Allama Mabani (rahimahullah).

---

## Sajdah Sahw -- Brief Explanation

- Sajdah Sahw is required when a **wajib** act is left out in prayer.
- **How it's done:** After the final Tashahud, do **one salam** (to the right only), then perform **two sajdahs**, then sit and read Tashahud and Durood again, then complete with both salams.
- If someone leaves a wajib and does Sajdah Sahw, the deficiency is compensated.
- If someone leaves a wajib and does **not** do Sajdah Sahw, the prayer is not valid.
