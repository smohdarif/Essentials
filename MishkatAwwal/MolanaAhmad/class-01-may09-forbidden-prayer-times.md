## Overview

This class covered the times when prayer is absolutely not permitted, the evidences behind them, and a major scholarly debate between the Hanafi and Shafi'i schools on whether missed fard prayers can be made up during these windows. The discussion went deep into how scholars handle conflicting ahadith, how Zawal is calculated, and an important lesson on intellectual honesty when defending your madhab.

---

## The Three Forbidden Times

You cannot pray salah during these three times:

### 1. Sunrise (Tulu' al-Shams)

When the sun is rising. Once tulu is done, the fuqaha say wait at least **10 minutes** before praying (this extra time is ikhtiyat -- precaution).

### 2. Midday (Zawal / Zaheera)

When the sun is standing straight -- right at its highest point before it starts declining. This is also called **nisf al-nahar** (half of the day).

- **Zaheera** means midday -- that's why **akl al-Zaheera** means lunch in Arabic
- Some linguists say **masa** (evening) actually starts right after Zawal
- Zawal itself is the **nisf** -- the exact halfway point of the day

### 3. Sunset (Ghurub)

When does ghurub start? When the sun becomes reddish and you can see it easily, and now it's going down -- it's hiding from us. Just right before it starts hiding -- that's the time of ghurub. You can already start feeling that it's time of Maghrib. That's the forbidden window.

Because there is **ta'jeel** (hastening) of Maghrib prayer, the ghurub window is much shorter -- only about **3-5 minutes**. The fuqaha say ghurub is the easiest of the three to manage.

## The Evidence: Hadith of Uqbah ibn Amir

The hadith states:

> **ثَلَاثُ سَاعَاتٍ كَانَ رَسُولُ اللهِ صَلَّى اللهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ يَنْهَانَا أَنْ نُصَلِّيَ فِيهِنَّ أَوْ أَنْ نَقْبُرَ فِيهِنَّ مَوْتَانَا: حِينَ تَطْلُعُ الشَّمْسُ بَازِغَةً حَتَّى تَرْتَفِعَ، وَحِينَ يَقُومُ قَائِمُ الظَّهِيرَةِ حَتَّى تَمِيلَ، وَحِينَ تَضَيَّفُ الشَّمْسُ لِلْغُرُوبِ حَتَّى تَغْرُبَ**

*"Three are the times the Messenger of Allah (peace be upon him) told us not to pray salah, and neither should we bury our deceased: when the sun is rising until it rises high; when it is standing straight (at its peak) until it declines; and when the sun starts to set until it completely disappears."*

### Breaking Down the Arabic

- **حِينَ تَطْلُعُ الشَّمْسُ بَازِغَةً** -- "when the sun is rising" -- بَازِغَةً means it's coming up
- **حَتَّى تَرْتَفِعَ** -- "until it rises high"
- **حِينَ يَقُومُ قَائِمُ الظَّهِيرَةِ** -- "when the sun is standing straight at midday" -- قَائِم means standing, الظَّهِيرَة is midday
- **حَتَّى تَمِيلَ** -- "until it declines/moves on" -- تَمِيلَ means to lean, decline, move to the other side
- **حِينَ تَضَيَّفُ الشَّمْسُ لِلْغُرُوبِ** -- "when the sun leans toward setting" -- تَضَيَّفُ means it starts to dawn/descend for setting
- **حَتَّى تَغْرُبَ** -- "until it completely sets/is hidden from us"

### What Does "Burying Our Dead" Mean Here?

The word **نَقْبُرَ** (from قَبَرَ -- to dig a grave/bury) -- but the **murad** (intended meaning) is not the physical act of putting someone in the ground. It means **Salat al-Janazah** (funeral prayer).

- **Dafan** (physical burial) is **not makruh** at these times
- **Salat al-Janazah** is makruh at these times
- **Sajdah al-Tilawah** is also makruh at these times

---

## What Else is Forbidden at These Times?

Both **Salat al-Janazah** and **Sajdat al-Tilawah** fall under the hukm of salah. Why?

Because Sajdat al-Tilawah requires **wudu** -- it has the **ishtiraat** (condition) of **taharat** (purity). Since it shares this condition with salah, it is considered from the **ajza' al-salah** (particles/components of prayer). So whatever applies to salah at these times also applies to these acts.

## How to Calculate Zawal

### The Old Method (What the Fuqaha Used to Do)

They would take a **stick** (let's say one meter tall) and stick it in the ground, then draw a circle around it. Then they'd watch the shadow:

1. In the morning, the shadow is long on one side
2. As the sun comes up, the shadow **contracts** (shrinks)
3. When the sun is directly overhead, the shadow is at its smallest
4. Now the sun starts moving to the other side -- it gives light from the opposite direction
5. So the shadow starts appearing on the **opposite side**
6. The fuqaha say: **when the shadow starts on the opposite side, Zawal is done**

### The Modern Method (Astronomy / Ilm al-Falakiyyat)

Using **longitudes** (Tul al-Balad) -- the imaginary lines running north-south on the globe:

- Latitudes = **Ard al-Balad** (horizontal lines)
- Longitudes = **Tul al-Balad** (vertical lines)
- Longitudes are drawn every **15 degrees** on the globe
- The sun takes **4 minutes** to pass from one longitude to the next
- 15 longitudes x 4 minutes = **60 minutes = 1 hour** -- that's how time zones work

### So How Long Does Zawal Actually Last?

**Only 4-6 minutes** according to the rules of falakiyyat (astronomy). Here's how:

- The actual Zawal (sun directly overhead) = **4 minutes**
- You add about **1 minute on either side** because being very near the center sometimes puts you in the same hukm
- That makes it about **6 minutes**
- But the fuqaha add even more for **ikhtiyat** (precaution), bringing it to about **10 minutes**

The extra surplus timings beyond the astronomical calculation are **only for ikhtiyat**. Usuli-wise, it should only be 4 minutes.

### The Country/Longitude Problem

Every country sits on specific longitudes:

- A small country like **Malawi** might cover only 3-4 longitudes
- A huge country like **China**, **Russia**, **Australia**, or **America** covers many longitudes
- But does everyone calculate their own Zawal based on their exact longitude? **No** -- that would be **haraj** (undue hardship) for the people
- Each country/city **chooses one official longitude** and bases its time on that

**Example -- China:** China is massive and covers maybe 15 longitudes. But Beijing shows only one time on the clock. On every longitude you're on a difference of 4 minutes. Even for prayer times, each area doesn't have its own separate timing -- that would be too difficult. The city picks one longitude and that's the standard.

**London/Greenwich** is where the global time system starts -- they made that their own system (the Prime Meridian).

## The Big Scholarly Disagreement

### Imam Shafi'i's Position

- In these three forbidden times, you **can** pray **qada fard salawat** (missed obligatory prayers)
- In **Makkah**, you can pray **any salah** at any time -- Zawal, Tulu, Ghurub -- any prayer is fine

### The Hanafi Position

- **No prayer at all** during these times -- not even missed fard, not even in Makkah
- The hadith (of Uqbah ibn Amir) in its general meaning is our **hujjah** (proof) **against Imam Shafi'i**

### Imam Abu Yusuf's Unique View

Imam Abu Yusuf says: **only on the day of Jumu'ah** (Friday) you are allowed to read **nawafil** (voluntary prayers) even during Zawal time. Why? Because of a narration that the Prophet (peace be upon him) or the Sahaba prayed Jumu'ah by Zawal time or before Zawal. **Imam Ahmad** (ibn Hanbal) also holds a similar view.

---

## The Strong Attack: The Sahih Bukhari Hadith

The opposition brings this hadith from Sahih Bukhari:

> **مَنْ أَدْرَكَ سَجْدَةً مِنْ صَلَاةِ الْفَجْرِ قَبْلَ أَنْ تَطْلُعَ الشَّمْسُ فَلْيُتِمَّ صَلَاتَهُ**

*"If anyone of you is able to get one sajdah (one rak'ah) of Salat al-Fajr before the sun rises, he should complete his salah."*

They say: **"This hadith is in Sahih Bukhari. The hadith has no defect in its chain. It is sahih. What is your madhab going to say now?"**

The teacher acknowledged: this is a **very straight attack on the Ahnaf**. It's a very strong ishkal (objection).

---

## The Hanafi Response -- "We Are Not Running Away"

The teacher emphasized: **"That's what people usually say -- the Ahnaf are running away. But we are NOT running away."**

### Answer 1: A Contradicting Hadith

This Bukhari hadith is contradicting **another hadith**. There are many ahadith that mention: when the Prophet (peace be upon him) said:

> When you see the **hamra** (redness, i.e., the redness of sunrise), **stop**. Because the sun is coming from the midst of the **horns of Shaytan/Iblis** -- like a shield going on.

So we are bringing the hadith from **Sahih Muslim** on our side. If they bring Imam Bukhari, we also have Imam Muslim.

### Answer 2: Reinterpreting the Bukhari Hadith

We are **not leaving** the Bukhari hadith. But what is its meaning? It means: **whoever catches the time of one rak'ah before Tulu -- that salah becomes wajib (obligatory) on him.**

Examples:
- A **woman** finishes her periods just before Tulu and she's able to bathe and pray -- Fajr becomes **fard on her**
- Someone becomes **baligh** (reaches puberty) at that time -- Fajr becomes fard on him
- A **kafir** becomes Muslim at that time -- that salah becomes wajib on him

The hadith isn't saying "pray during sunrise" -- it's saying **"the obligation is now on you"** (and you pray it after the forbidden time passes).

---

## The Even Heavier Ishkal: The Inconsistency Accusation

The opposition makes a **second, even stronger** objection:

> *"You Ahnaf are taking the hadith the way you want! The hadith mentions BOTH Fajr (sunrise) AND Asr (sunset). For Fajr: you say if someone starts praying and Tulu al-Shams comes, his salah becomes fasid -- he must break it by saying salam (whether standing or wherever he is) and sit down. He has to repeat it later. But for Asr: you say if someone is reading Asr of THAT DAY (not qada, but that day's Asr) and the time of Ghurub comes, it's fine -- he can continue.*
>
> *You are taking the first portion of the hadith but not the second portion. Who gave you the authority to take half of the hadith and leave the other half?"*

The teacher said: **"This ishkal is very heavy."**

### Practical Scenario at Fajr Time

The teacher described what happens practically if it's close to sunrise:
- You say takbir, you don't even stop in between
- The fard is only just to be in the ruku position -- he's just going up and down
- **It shouldn't be like the way a hen/chicken pecks on the ground** -- that is also **manhiyyun anhu** (forbidden)
- This is in the time of emergency
- If you also have sunnah to read, you can make one of them short (the sunnah) and read the fard properly, depending on how much time you have

### The Hanafi Response

We say: **we are making jama' (gathering/reconciliation) between both ahadith.** We are not leaving any hadith. When they tell us one hadith from Imam Bukhari, we bring the hadith from Sahih Muslim. Both ahadith are sahih.

---

## Ta'arud: When Two Sahih Ahadith Clash

Now we have a situation called:

**Ta'arud (تَعَارُض)** = You have two ahadith of the **same level of strength**, and you don't have anything to give tarjih (preference) to one over the other. Both are high level. You can't prefer one on top of the other.

### The Resolution Procedure (Step by Step)

**Step 1: Tatbiq (تَطْبِيق) -- Reconciliation**

First, try to **gather both ahadith together** in the mas'alah -- find a way to make both work. This is what the Ahnaf did here: the Bukhari hadith is interpreted as establishing obligation (wujub), while the Muslim hadith is taken at face value for stopping prayer. This is called **wajh al-jam'** (the method of gathering).

If tatbiq works -- well and good. If not, move to Step 2.

**Step 2: Tarjih (تَرْجِيح) -- Preference**

Can we give tarjih (preference) to one hadith over the other? In this case: **no**. Why? Because some ulama have given tarjih to Sahih Muslim, and some ulama say Sahih Bukhari is on top. So they are both considered equal -- neither can overcome the other.

If tarjih is not possible, move to Step 3.

**Step 3: Naskh (نَسْخ) -- Abrogation**

If we can't bond the two ahadith together and we can't prefer one over the other, we look at which came **later in time**:
- The later hadith is the **Nasikh (نَاسِخ)** -- the one that is acted upon
- The earlier hadith is the **Mansukh (مَنْسُوخ)** -- the one that is no longer acted upon

**How do we know which came later?** There are many **asbab** (methods) in Ulum al-Hadith:
- One method: look at which Sahabi **entered Islam later** and which one earlier
- Another: which Sahabi was there with the Prophet (peace be upon him) for how long and in how many occasions
- Based on these factors, you determine which hadith to make nasikh and which mansukh

---

## Muttafaqun Alayh: What Does It Mean?

**Muttafaqun Alayh (مُتَّفَقٌ عَلَيْهِ)** -- "agreed upon."

When you see a hadith and then see "Muttafaqun Alayh" written after it, it means:

- **Both** Imam Bukhari **and** Imam Muslim have narrated that riwayah in their respective books
- The word **ittifaq** means **agreement** -- they've both agreed on that hadith
- In the terms of Ulum al-Hadith, this is a **very, very high level** of hadith

### Important Nuances

**Usul al-Hadith is ijtihadi, just like Usul al-Fiqh:**

- **Usul al-Fiqh** is **mujtahad fi** (derived through ijtihad) -- it is not **mansus** (you don't get the exact words directly from Quran and Sunnah). The fuqaha made ijtihad in the nusus (texts) and brought out usul (principles).
- **Likewise**, the Aimmah of Hadith did the same. They made ittifaq that Sahih Bukhari and Sahih Muslim are the top category -- but this was according to the Aimmah of Hadith, not something directly from revelation.

**The ijma came later:**

- The ijma (consensus) of the Ummah, the qabuliyyat (acceptance) of Bukhari and Muslim being the highest -- this was not established during the lifetime of Imam Bukhari and Imam Muslim. It was done **later on**.
- Since the entire **Ummati Muhammadiyyah** has taken it with **qabul** (acceptance), it is now considered **ijma'**.
- If someone now comes and says a hadith in these collections is **da'if** (weak) or **matruk** (abandoned), **it will not be listened to** -- because the Ummah has already done ijma'.

## The Ta'assub Warning -- A Critical Lesson

This was one of the most important points of the class. The teacher gave a strong warning about scholarly bias:

### The Problem

When it comes to **nusrat of madhab** (supporting your school of thought), there's a bad habit that should not be there:

- When a hadith supporting **our** istidlal (evidence) comes in Sahih Bukhari, we tell the opposition: *"See! Our hadith is in Sahih Bukhari!"*
- But when the **opposition's** hadith is also in Sahih Bukhari, we say: *"So what? Being in Sahih Bukhari doesn't mean it can overcome every other hadith."*

The teacher said plainly: **"This is a big type of ta'assub. This ta'assub is not nice."**

### The Comparison with Imam Malik's Muwatta

To drive the point home, the teacher pointed out:

- Imam Malik's Muwatta has **very few ruwaat** (narrators) in its chains -- sometimes only **3 narrators** reaching back to the Prophet (peace be upon him)
- Imam Bukhari's chains reach **5-6 narrators**
- Fewer narrators = shorter chain = theoretically stronger
- So by that measure, the Muwatta should be considered even more reliable

If we're willing to use "it's in Sahih Bukhari!" as a weapon for our side but then dismiss the same argument when the other side uses it -- **that's ta'assub**, and it's not acceptable.

### The Correct Approach

- Everything should be based on **daleel** (evidence)
- When we have a **qareena** (supporting indication) that our daleel is in Sahih Bukhari, we can use it as a **hujjah** (proof)
- But we should **never neglect** the opposition's daleel from Sahih Bukhari **without a valid daleel** of our own
- Since we are now reading **Fiqh with Dala'il** (jurisprudence with evidences), we should be:
  - **Mutasalliq** (firm and strong in our position) -- YES
  - **Muta'assib** (blindly fanatical, ignoring the other side even when they're correct) -- NO
- **Tasallul** should be there. **Ta'assub** should not.

## Quick Summary

- **Three forbidden prayer times:** Tulu' al-Shams (sunrise), Zawal (midday), Ghurub (sunset)
- **You CAN bury the dead** at these times -- but **don't pray Janazah or do Sajdah Tilawah**
- **Zawal lasts only 4-6 minutes** astronomically; fuqaha add ikhtiyat making it ~10 minutes
- **Hanafi view:** No prayer at all during these times -- not even qada fard, not even in Makkah
- **Imam Shafi'i:** Allows qada fard during these times; allows all prayer in Makkah at any time
- **Imam Abu Yusuf:** Allows nawafil during Zawal only on Jumu'ah (Friday)
- **The big attack:** Sahih Bukhari hadith seems to allow completing Fajr at sunrise
- **Hanafi answers:** (1) Counter with Sahih Muslim hadith about stopping at redness, (2) Reinterpret Bukhari hadith as establishing obligation, not permission
- **The heavier attack:** Ahnaf accused of taking half the hadith (blocking Fajr at sunrise but allowing Asr at sunset)
- **Response:** Jama' (reconciliation) between both ahadith using tatbiq
- **Ta'arud resolution procedure:** Tatbiq --> Tarjih --> Naskh/Mansukh
- **Critical lesson:** Be mutasalliq (firm), not muta'assib (fanatical) -- intellectual honesty is mandatory
