## Sitting Positions: Iftirash vs Tawarruk

The teacher briefly mentioned two sitting positions in prayer:

- **First Qa'dah (Tashahud after 2 rakats):** **Iftirash** -- sitting on the left foot
- **Second Qa'dah (Final Tashahud):** **Tawarruk** -- taking out both legs from one side and sitting on the ground directly

---

## Hadith: Raising Hands for Takbir

From the narration of Ibn Khuzaymah:

When the Prophet (peace be upon him) stood for prayer and raised both his hands:
- They were **in line with his shoulders**
- He **lined both thumbs with both ears**
- Then he did takbir -- this is how the Ahnaf (Hanafis) do it

**"Shahmah"** = the **lower soft part of the ear** (the earlobe)

In another narration: he raised his thumbs till they lined with the **lower soft part (earlobe)** of the ear.

---

## Hadith: Holding Hands in Qiyam

The Prophet (peace be upon him) used to **hold his left hand with his right hand** during qiyam (standing in prayer). This is the standard Hanafi practice.

---

## Hadith: The Man Who Prayed Incorrectly (Additional Details)

This is the same hadith discussed in previous classes, but with additional wording and details from other narrations:

The Prophet (peace be upon him) taught the man:

1. Face the Qibla
2. Say the takbir
3. Read **Surah al-Fatiha** (referred to here as **Umm al-Quran** -- "the Mother of the Quran")
4. Read whatever else Allah wishes you to read
5. Put **both palms on both knees** in ruku
6. **Settle yourself in ruku** -- spread the back straight and flat
7. Rise and **make the back upright**, raise the head
8. Stay until **the bones return to their joints** (ta'dil al-arkan)
9. **Settle in sajdah** properly
10. When rising, **sit on the left leg** (iftirash)
11. Do this in **every ruku and every sajdah** until you are at ease

### An Alternative Version for Those Who Don't Know Quran

Another narration of the same hadith adds: if you stand for prayer and have done wudu as commanded, then:
- If you have memorized some Quran -- read it
- If you **don't** memorize anything from the Quran -- then say:
  - **الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ** (Alhamdulillah)
  - **اللهُ أَكْبَرُ** (Allahu Akbar)
  - **لَا إِلٰهَ إِلَّا اللهُ** (La ilaha illallah)
  - Then do ruku

The teacher noted that further discussion on what exactly must be read after takbir (the thana) would come in the ahadith ahead.

## Hadith: Prayer is "Mathna Mathna" (Two by Two)

The hadith states that prayer (nawafil) is **mathna mathna** -- in sets of two:
- Tashahud in every two rakats
- Prayer must have **khushu'** (humility), **tamaskkun** (stillness/composure)
- Pray with ease
- After finishing, raise both hands for du'a with palms facing the face and say du'a
- Whoever doesn't do this -- his prayer is incomplete, without barakah

### The Scholarly Differences on Nawafil Rakats

**Imam Malik's view:**
- At night, a person **cannot** pray 4 rakats with one salam
- Must do **2 and 2** -- salam after every 2 rakats
- Based on this hadith "mathna mathna"

**Other scholars' view:**
- You can pray as many as you want without salam in between: 4, 6, 8, 10
- Just do tashahud after every 2 rakats and stand up to continue

**Imam Shafi'i's view:**
- Whether at night or during the day, praying **2-2 rakats** is more virtuous (afḍal)

**Imam Abu Yusuf and Imam Muhammad (Sahibayn):**
- At **night**: praying 2-2 rakats is more preferable
- During the **day**: praying 4-4 rakats is more preferable

**Imam Abu Hanifah (Imam A'zam):**
- Praying **4 rakats** at a time -- whether day or night -- is more preferable

## Hadith of Abu Sa'eed al-Khudri (may Allah be pleased with him)

Abu Sa'eed al-Khudri led them in prayer. He said the **takbir loudly**:
- When raising his head from sajdah
- When going into sajdah
- When rising from the two rakats (for the second set)

He said:

> **هٰكَذَا رَأَيْتُ النَّبِيَّ صَلَّى اللهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ**

*"This is how I saw the Prophet (peace be upon him) pray."*

## Hadith: 22 Takbirs in Prayer -- The Sunnah of Abil Qasim

Ikrimah narrated that he prayed behind an **old sheikh** (an elderly man) in Makkah. That sheikh was **not saying takbir** at every movement (going up and down).

Ikrimah said to him: **"Ahmaq!"** (Foolish!) -- and used the Arabic expression meaning "May your mother lose you" (a strong expression of disapproval). Then he said:

> **هٰذِهِ سُنَّةُ أَبِي الْقَاسِمِ صَلَّى اللهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ**

*"This is the Sunnah of Abil Qasim (the Prophet, peace be upon him)."*

Meaning: saying takbir at **every up and down movement** is the sunnah. The total comes to **22 takbirs** in a standard 4-rakat prayer.

### A Note on Hadith Terminology: Mursal

Since Ikrimah is a **Tabi'i** (he did not meet the Prophet directly), when he says "This is the Sunnah of the Prophet" without naming which Sahabi he heard it from -- this is called **Mursal**. One Sahabi is **mahdhuf** (omitted) from the chain. The Tabi'i is narrating without mentioning the Sahabi's name.

## Major Hadith: Ibn Mas'ud's Prayer -- Proof Against Raf' al-Yadayn

This is one of the most important ahadith discussed in this class.

Abdullah ibn Mas'ud (may Allah be pleased with him) said:

> **أَلَا أُصَلِّي لَكُمْ صَلَاةَ رَسُولِ اللهِ صَلَّى اللهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ؟**

*"Shall I not pray for you the prayer of the Messenger of Allah (peace be upon him)?"*

So he prayed -- and **he did NOT raise his hands except once**, at the **Takbirat al-Tahrimah** (the opening takbir only). After that, he did not raise his hands at any other point in the prayer.

### Why This is So Important

The teacher emphasized: **This is one of the very big dalils (proofs) of the Ahnaf** for the position that there is **no raf' al-yadayn** (raising of hands) except at the opening takbir.

- The hadith is found in the **Sahihayn** (both Sahih Bukhari and Sahih Muslim) -- though Abu Dawud (rahimahullah) also narrates it
- Ibn Mas'ud (may Allah be pleased with him) was one of the closest Companions to the Prophet (peace be upon him) and knew his prayer intimately
- He specifically demonstrated: raf' al-yadayn is **only at the Takbirah al-Tahrimah** -- not at ruku, not at rising from ruku, not anywhere else

## Hadith: The Prophet Could See Behind Him (A Miracle)

The Prophet (peace be upon him) saw a person making mistakes in prayer and told him:

> *"Don't you fear Allah? Don't you see how you pray? Do you think what you do is hidden from me? By the grace of Allah, I can see behind me just as I see in front of me."*

The teacher noted: this is one of the **Mu'jizat** (miracles) of the Prophet (peace be upon him) -- that while praying, he could see what was happening behind him as well.

## Chapter: What is Read After the Takbir? (The Opening Du'a / Thana)

This was a major section of the class. After saying "Allahu Akbar" to open the prayer, what do you recite before Surah al-Fatiha?

### Three Scholarly Views

**1. Imam Malik:**
- **Nothing** is read before Fatiha
- After saying Allahu Akbar, go directly to:

> **الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ**

No thana, no opening du'a.

**2. Imam Shafi'i:**
- Read the ayah from **Surah al-An'am (6:79)** (in the 7th Juz):

> **إِنِّي وَجَّهْتُ وَجْهِيَ لِلَّذِي فَطَرَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ حَنِيفًا وَمَا أَنَا مِنَ الْمُشْرِكِينَ**

*"Indeed, I have turned my face toward the One who created the heavens and the earth, inclining toward truth, and I am not of the polytheists."*

**3. Imam Abu Hanifah, Imam Muhammad, and Imam Ahmad ibn Hanbal:**
- Read the **Thana** (which is what we all commonly recite):

> **سُبْحَانَكَ اللَّهُمَّ وَبِحَمْدِكَ وَتَبَارَكَ اسْمُكَ وَتَعَالَى جَدُّكَ وَلَا إِلٰهَ غَيْرُكَ**

*"Glory be to You, O Allah, and praise be to You. Blessed is Your name, and exalted is Your majesty. There is no god besides You."*

## Hadith: The Quiet Moment Between Takbir and Recitation

Abu Hurairah (may Allah be pleased with him) narrated that the Prophet (peace be upon him) used to be **quiet for a small period** between the takbir and the qira'ah (recitation of Fatiha).

The narrators differed slightly on the wording -- some said **"hunayya"** (a very small moment) and others said **"hunayha"** (slightly different pronunciation) -- but the meaning is the same: a brief pause.

Abu Hurairah asked the Prophet (peace be upon him):

> *"My parents be sacrificed for you -- what do you say in that quietness between the takbir and the recitation?"*

The Prophet (peace be upon him) replied:

> **اللَّهُمَّ بَاعِدْ بَيْنِي وَبَيْنَ خَطَايَايَ كَمَا بَاعَدْتَ بَيْنَ الْمَشْرِقِ وَالْمَغْرِبِ**

*"O Allah, distance me from my sins as You have distanced the East (Mashriq) from the West (Maghrib)."*

> **اللَّهُمَّ نَقِّنِي مِنْ خَطَايَايَ كَمَا يُنَقَّى الثَّوْبُ الْأَبْيَضُ مِنَ الدَّنَسِ**

*"O Allah, purify me from my sins as a white garment is purified from dirt."*

> **اللَّهُمَّ اغْسِلْنِي مِنْ خَطَايَايَ بِالْمَاءِ وَالثَّلْجِ وَالْبَرَدِ**

*"O Allah, wash my sins from me with water, snow (thalj), and hail (barad)."*

### A Note on Print Editions

The teacher pointed out a difference between editions:
- The **Bushra edition** (from Pakistan) does not have the word **الْبَرَدِ** (hail) in this hadith
- The **Maktaba Islami edition** (from Beirut) does include it
- This is because different editions are based on different manuscripts (nusakh) of the students of the original author
- The teacher reassured: **this is something normal** in Islamic books and nothing to worry about

**Vocabulary:**
- **الثَّلْجِ** (al-thalj) = snow
- **الْبَرَدِ** (al-barad) = hail (the small pieces of ice that come from clouds during rain) -- it is very white, which is why it's used as a metaphor for purification

## Hadith: The Long Opening Du'a of the Prophet (peace be upon him)

When the Prophet (peace be upon him) stood for prayer and opened it with takbir, he would say:

> **إِنِّي وَجَّهْتُ وَجْهِيَ لِلَّذِي فَطَرَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ حَنِيفًا وَمَا أَنَا مِنَ الْمُشْرِكِينَ**

*"I have turned my face to the One who created the heavens and the earth, inclining to truth, and I am not of the polytheists."*

> **إِنَّ صَلَاتِي وَنُسُكِي وَمَحْيَايَ وَمَمَاتِي لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ ، لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ ، وَبِذٰلِكَ أُمِرْتُ وَأَنَا مِنَ الْمُسْلِمِينَ**

*"My prayer, my sacrifice, my life, and my death are for Allah, Lord of all the worlds. He has no partner. And for that I have been commanded, and I am of the Muslims."*

> **اللَّهُمَّ أَنْتَ الْمَلِكُ لَا إِلٰهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ ، أَنْتَ رَبِّي وَأَنَا عَبْدُكَ ، ظَلَمْتُ نَفْسِي وَاعْتَرَفْتُ بِذَنْبِي فَاغْفِرْ لِي ذُنُوبِي جَمِيعًا ، إِنَّهُ لَا يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ**

*"O Allah, You are the King, there is no god but You. You are my Lord and I am Your servant. I have wronged myself and I have acknowledged my sins, so forgive all my sins -- for no one forgives sins except You."*

> **وَاهْدِنِي لِأَحْسَنِ الْأَخْلَاقِ ، لَا يَهْدِي لِأَحْسَنِهَا إِلَّا أَنْتَ ، وَاصْرِفْ عَنِّي سَيِّئَهَا ، لَا يَصْرِفُ عَنِّي سَيِّئَهَا إِلَّا أَنْتَ**

*"Guide me to the best of manners -- no one guides to the best of them except You. And turn away from me bad manners -- no one turns them away except You."*

> **لَبَّيْكَ وَسَعْدَيْكَ ، وَالْخَيْرُ كُلُّهُ فِي يَدَيْكَ ، وَالشَّرُّ لَيْسَ إِلَيْكَ**

*"I stand before You in devotion and obedience. All good is in Your hands, and evil is not attributed to You."*

---

## Important Aqeedah Point: Evil is Not Attributed to Allah

The teacher paused on the phrase **وَالشَّرُّ لَيْسَ إِلَيْكَ** ("evil is not attributed to You") and explained an important point of belief:

- **Allah is the Creator (Khaliq) of everything** -- both good and evil. He created them both.
- **But out of adab (respect and propriety)**, we do not attribute bad things directly to Allah. We don't do **mansoob** (attribution) of evil to Him.

### The Example of Ibrahim (peace be upon him)

The teacher brought the example from **Surah al-Shu'ara (26:78-80)** where Ibrahim (peace be upon him) speaks about Allah:

For all the **good things**, he attributed them directly to Allah:
- **الَّذِي خَلَقَنِي فَهُوَ يَهْدِينِ** -- *"He who created me, He guides me"*
- **وَالَّذِي هُوَ يُطْعِمُنِي وَيَسْقِينِ** -- *"And He who feeds me and gives me drink"*

But when it came to **sickness** (something unpleasant), Ibrahim said:

> **وَإِذَا مَرِضْتُ فَهُوَ يَشْفِينِ**

*"And when **I** get sick, **He** cures me."*

He said "when **I** get sick" -- he attributed the sickness **to himself**, not to Allah. He did **not** say "when He makes me sick." But for the cure, he attributed it back to Allah: "He cures me."

This is **adab with Allah** -- a beautiful lesson in how to speak about Allah, even though we know He is the ultimate Creator of all things.

## Quick Summary

- **Iftirash** (first qa'dah) vs **Tawarruk** (final qa'dah) -- two different sitting positions
- **Hands for takbir:** raised to shoulder level, thumbs aligned with earlobes (shahmah)
- **Hold left hand with right** during qiyam
- **Man who prayed incorrectly:** additional details -- palms on knees in ruku, back straight, sit on left leg, every ruku and sajdah with ease
- **If you don't know Quran:** say Alhamdulillah, Allahu Akbar, La ilaha illallah, then ruku
- **Nawafil are "mathna mathna"** (2 by 2): Imam Malik says must do 2-2 at night; Imam A'zam prefers 4 rakats day or night
- **22 takbirs** in a 4-rakat prayer -- saying takbir at every movement is sunnah
- **Ibn Mas'ud's hadith: biggest dalil for no raf' al-yadayn** -- raised hands only at Takbirat al-Tahrimah
- **Prophet's miracle:** could see behind him while praying
- **Three views on opening du'a:** Imam Malik (nothing), Imam Shafi'i (Surah al-An'am 6:79), Imam Abu Hanifah/Imam Ahmad (Subhanaka/Thana)
- **Du'a between takbir and Fatiha:** "O Allah, distance me from my sins..." with water, snow, and hail
- **Long opening du'a** includes wajjahtu, salati wa nusuki, Allahumma antal malik, and ihdini li-ahsanil akhlaq
- **Aqeedah lesson:** Evil is not attributed to Allah out of adab -- Ibrahim's example in Surah al-Shu'ara
