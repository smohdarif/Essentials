# Class Notes: Wajib Li-Aynihi vs Li-Ghayrihi & Bab al-Adhan - May 16th 2026

*A simplified summary of the lecture covering the types of wajib in makruh times, additional makruh scenarios, the transition to Kitab al-Salah, and the chapter on Adhan*

---

## Part 1: Continuing from Makruh Prayer Times -- Why is Karahah There?

The teacher clarified a very important point: the **karahah** (dislike) of praying nawafil during the forbidden times is **due to the right of fard** (haq al-fard) -- it is **not due to the time itself**.

Why does this matter? Because if you say the karahah is due to the time, then other fard prayers also exist in certain times, and you'd have to say they can't be read either -- which doesn't work. So the karahah is linked to the right of the fard prayer, not to the clock.

That is why:
- The karahah **does not apply** to fard prayers
- The karahah **does not apply** to something that is wajib in itself (wajib li-aynihi)

---

## The Two Types of Wajib

This was a key distinction the teacher explained:

### 1. Wajib li-Aynihi (Wajib in Itself)

This is something that **Sharia itself** has made wajib. It's obligatory on its own -- not because of any human action or vow.

**Examples:**
- **Salat al-Janazah** -- when there's a janazah, it's wajib from Sharia. You didn't make it wajib; Allah made it wajib.
- **Sajdah al-Tilawah** -- it's Quranic, from Sharia itself. It's wajib on its own.

These **can be performed** during the forbidden times (like sunrise and sunset) because their wujub comes directly from Sharia.

**Practical example:** When you're in Umrah or in Makkah/Madinah, after almost every salah they announce a janazah prayer. If it falls during a makruh time, you still pray it -- because Salat al-Janazah is wajib li-aynihi.

### 2. Wajib li-Ghayrihi (Wajib Due to Something Else)

This is something that became wajib **because of the person's own action** -- not from Sharia directly.

**Examples:**
- A person makes a **nadhr** (vow): "I vow to fast the next 12 months." This fasting didn't become wajib because of Sharia -- the person made it wajib on himself through his own vow.
- **Qasam** (oath) or **halif** (swearing) -- same idea. The person creates the obligation himself.
- A person starts a **nafil** prayer and then breaks it -- now repeating it becomes wajib. But that wujub came from the person's own action (starting and then breaking the nafil), not from Sharia.

These are **NOT allowed** during the forbidden times. The karahah applies to them because their wujub is from the person, not from Allah's direct command.

## A Tricky Question: What About Rakatayn al-Tawaf and Sajdah al-Tilawah?

A student raised a sharp question: if the two rakats after tawaf become wajib because of the person doing tawaf (his own action), and Sajdah al-Tilawah also becomes wajib when a person recites -- aren't both dependent on the person's action (fi'l)? Why differentiate?

The teacher worked through this:

### Sajdah al-Tilawah

- A person does it or doesn't do it -- it's wajib regardless. Even if **someone else** recites the ayah and you happen to hear it, Sajdah becomes wajib on you.
- This is **wajib li-aynihi** -- the wujub is from Sharia.

### Rakatayn al-Tawaf (2 Rakats after Tawaf)

- The wujub is **mawquf** (dependent/suspended) on the person's own action.
- If the person doesn't do tawaf, the 2 rakats never become wajib.
- If the person doesn't finish the tawaf, the wujub doesn't come.
- You need to **protect your ibadah from butlan** (invalidity) -- keep it valid until the end. This is in your hands, in your control.

### Breaking a Nafil Prayer

- Nafil remains nafil until a person breaks it. Even while you're praying it, the hukm is nafil.
- The wujub of repeating it **does not come** until the person himself breaks it.
- So the wujub was created by the person's own action -- making it wajib li-ghayrihi.

### Salat al-Janazah

- This is **not in your hand**. It's not dependent on your action -- someone passed away and Sharia obligated you to pray.

The teacher admitted he still had some doubt on the finer details and said he would look further into it.

## Why is Sajdah al-Tilawah Wajib? The Deeper Reason

The teacher explained the wisdom behind it:

Sajdah al-Tilawah is wajib **mostly at those places in the Quran where Shaytan refused to make sajdah**, or where the **Mushrikeen denied making sajdah**. So it becomes wajib for us to differentiate ourselves from them.

**The famous hadith:** The Prophet (peace be upon him) recited an ayah of sajdah. All the Sahaba made sajdah. But Abu Sufyan (or another person who was not yet Muslim at the time, still a mushrik) -- instead of making sajdah -- just **took some sand and put it on his forehead**. So to make the distinction clear, we must do proper sajdah.

**Another example from the Quran:** When the mushrikeen were told to make sajdah to Allah, they responded:

> "Who is al-Rahman?"

And they **refused** to do sajdah. So we must do sajdah at those places to show we are not like them.

## Can You Skip an Ayah of Sajdah While Reading Quran?

A student asked this. The teacher's answer:

- **No**, you shouldn't skip it. Skipping it is **makruh** -- it's like dodging an obligation.
- Read Quran properly in **tartil** (measured, understood pace).
- If you read the ayah, the sajdah becomes wajib. Don't try to avoid it by skipping.
- The teacher noted this could be another reason why Sajdah al-Tilawah is considered wajib li-aynihi -- because when reading Quran in order, you **can't skip** the ayah, so the sajdah will come either way. It's not in your choice.

---

## Can You Delay Sajdah al-Tilawah?

A student asked if you must do it immediately. The answer:

- **No**, it's not required to do it right away.
- You can delay it.
- But you should still do it -- don't just forget about it.

## Additional Makruh Times for Prayer

The teacher quickly covered a few more scenarios:

### 1. More Than 2 Rakats of Fajr Sunnah

It is **makruh** to read more than two rakats of Fajr sunnah after subh sadiq (true dawn). Why? Because the Prophet (peace be upon him) **did not read more than that** -- even though he was very keen and determined to read as much salah as possible. If even he didn't do it, we shouldn't either.

### 2. No Nafil After Ghurub / Before Maghrib Fard

You cannot read nafil salah after sunset (ghurub) before praying the fard of Maghrib. Why? Because there is **ta'jeel** (hastening) of Maghrib -- delaying Maghrib is not permissible, and reading nafil before it would cause delay.

### 3. No Nafil During the Khutbah of Jumu'ah

You cannot read nafil salah when the **Imam has come out for khutbah** on the day of Jumu'ah -- not until he finishes the khutbah. Why? Because it is **wajib to listen to the khutbah**, and reading nafil during that time means getting yourself busy with something else when listening is obligatory.

## Part 2: Transition to Kitab al-Salah -- Why This Order?

The teacher explained the logic of how the Hidaya is organized:

### Why Tahara (Purity) Came First

One of the **main sharait** (conditions) of Salah is **Tahara**. No matter what happens, Tahara is always required. It is **never** excluded from the Sharia of Salah:
- If you can't do **wudu**, you do **tayammum**
- If you can't do **ghusl**, you do **tayammum**
- Tahara is **never compromised**

That's why the discussion of Tahara came first in the book. (Tahara will come again later but not in as much detail.)

### Why Timings Came Next

After Tahara, the book discussed the **timings of Salah**. Why? Because the **sabab** (cause) of the **wujub** (obligation) of Salah is the **time**. If the time doesn't exist, Salah doesn't become wajib.

**Interesting example:** There are countries that barely see night -- the sun sets and almost immediately rises again, with no real period of darkness.

- **Sahib al-Kanz** says: Isha and Maghrib would **not become fard** on a person living in such a place. Why? Because the time condition isn't met.
- **Other Ulama** say: this person should look at the **nearby countries**, see what timings they follow, and make calculations based on that to determine his prayer times.

### Why Adhan Comes Now

So the flow is: Tahara (you're pure) → Timings (salah is now wajib on you, and you know when you can and can't pray) → now **how do you call people to come pray?** → That's **Bab al-Adhan**.

## Bab al-Adhan (Chapter on the Call to Prayer)

### What Does Adhan Mean?

**Adhan** literally means: **to let someone know / to inform / to announce**.

### For Which Prayers is Adhan Given?

Adhan is **mashroo'** (legislated) for **five daily prayers and Jumu'ah only**. Not for any other prayer -- not Eid, not Taraweeh, nothing else.

### The Proof: Tawatur Amali

The proof is **Tawatur Amali** (continuous unbroken practice). The teacher explained that Tawatur has two types:

1. **Tawatur Naqli** -- transmitted through narrations (chains of hadith)
2. **Tawatur Amali** -- continuous practice/action passed down generation to generation

**Amal (practice) at times is more valuable and heavier** than a hadith which is Khabr Wahid or Mashhur but not Mutawatir. The continuous Amal of the Ummah -- from the time of the Prophet (peace be upon him) till today, century after century -- is that adhan has **only** been given for the five daily prayers and Jumu'ah.

### What About the Adhan for Tahajjud?

There was one adhan that was given for Tahajjud -- but this was **not** an ilam (announcement) for any fard prayer. It was just a **signal/alarm** for those Sahaba who were reading Tahajjud from the beginning of the night. They would go to sleep in the middle of the night, and then the next group would wake up and read. The adhan was just to signal this shift. This is clearly stated in the hadith.

---

## How the Adhan Was Established: The Story

The Prophet (peace be upon him) gathered the Sahaba and held a meeting. The question was: **How do we call people to Salah?**

### Three Rejected Suggestions

1. **Light a fire** so people see the smoke and know it's time → **Rejected** -- because that's what the **Majus** (fire-worshippers/Zoroastrians) do.

2. **Ring a bell** → **Rejected** -- because that's the way of the **Nasara** (Christians). The Prophet (peace be upon him) didn't like it.

3. **Blow a horn** → **Rejected** -- because that's the way of the **Yahud** (Jews). There was **mushababahat** (resemblance) to their practice.

### The Dream

Then one or two Sahaba saw a dream. One of them was **Abdullah ibn Abd Rabbih** (may Allah be pleased with him).

He was thinking about what should be done. In his dream, an **angel (Farishta)** came down from the skies and sat with him. The angel asked: *"What are you thinking about? What's your worry?"*

He told the angel about needing something to call people to salah. The angel said:

> *"Should I not teach you some kalimat (words)?"*

He said: *"Yes, why not?"*

So the angel taught him the **entire adhan**.

**Ibn Hajar** (rahimahullah) has narrated the full account of this.

The **tartib** (order) of the Adhan follows exactly the way the angel recited it.

---

## Tarji' in Adhan -- Do We Do It?

### What is Tarji'?

**Tarji'** means: a person says the shahadatayn first with a **lower voice**, then repeats them with a **louder voice**. So:

1. **أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلٰهَ إِلَّا اللهُ** (low voice)
2. **أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلٰهَ إِلَّا اللهُ** (loud voice)
3. **أَشْهَدُ أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا رَّسُولُ اللهِ** (low voice)
4. **أَشْهَدُ أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا رَّسُولُ اللهِ** (loud voice)

So in total it would be **four times each** -- once low, once loud, again low, again loud.

### The Hanafi Position: No Tarji'

**We don't do tarji'.** The shahadatayn are said twice each in a normal voice -- no low-then-loud alternation.

### The Hadith of Abu Mahzurah (may Allah be pleased with him)

Abu Mahzurah narrated that the Prophet (peace be upon him) commanded him to give adhan in this way. But the Hanafi answer to this is clear:

- Abu Mahzurah was **very young** at the time and was **playing around** -- imitating the adhan as a joke
- The Prophet (peace be upon him) caught him and said: *"Can you repeat what you were saying?"*
- Abu Mahzurah said the words but with a **very low voice** (out of shyness/nervousness)
- The Prophet (peace be upon him) told him: **"Say it with a louder voice"**
- So he said it louder

Abu Mahzurah **understood this as tarji'** -- that you need to say it first low and then loud. But actually, **the Prophet (peace be upon him) was just teaching him** to speak up. It was a teaching moment, not a legislative command.

**Proof:** There is **no tarji'** in the adhan of **any other Sahabi** -- not in the adhan of Abdullah (ibn Abd Rabbih), and not in the adhan of **Bilal** (may Allah be pleased with him). Abu Mahzurah continued doing it his way because that's how he understood the Prophet's instruction, but no one else adopted it.

## "Al-Salatu Khayrun min al-Nawm" -- Special to Fajr Adhan

After **حَيَّ عَلَى الْفَلَاحِ** (Hayya ala al-Falah), the muadhin says in the **Fajr adhan only**:

> **الصَّلَاةُ خَيْرٌ مِنَ النَّوْمِ** (x2)

*"Prayer is better than sleep."*

### Why?

Because **Bilal** (may Allah be pleased with him) used to say this. The hadith mentions that the Prophet (peace be upon him) was found lying down/sleeping, and Bilal came to call him and said these words. The Prophet (peace be upon him) responded:

> *"How beautiful, how wonderful these words are! Put them in your Fajr adhan."*

These kalimat are **specific to the Fajr adhan only** -- because Fajr is the time of nawm (sleep), so the reminder that prayer is better than sleep is particularly fitting.

## Q&A: What About Prayer During Heavy Rain?

A student asked whether something extra is added to the adhan when it's raining.

The teacher clarified: **Nothing is added to the adhan itself.** Rather, a separate announcement is made:

> **صَلُّوا فِي رِحَالِكُمْ**

*"Pray in your camps/homes."*

This happened in the era of the Sahaba when it was raining heavily. The Prophet (peace be upon him) told them to make this announcement so people wouldn't risk getting fully drenched or **slipping and falling** while rushing to the masjid.

Heavy rain is one of the recognized **excuses ('udhr)** for not attending congregational prayer in the masjid. But this announcement is **not** a special addition to the adhan -- it's a separate statement.

## Quick Summary

- **Karahah in forbidden times** is due to the right of fard (haq al-fard), not due to the time itself
- **Wajib li-Aynihi** (from Sharia: Janazah, Sajdah Tilawah) -- allowed in forbidden times
- **Wajib li-Ghayrihi** (from person's own action: nadhr, broken nafil) -- NOT allowed in forbidden times
- **Sajdah Tilawah is wajib** to distinguish us from Shaytan and the Mushrikeen who refused to prostrate
- **Don't skip** ayat of sajdah while reading Quran -- that's makruh
- **Additional makruh times:** more than 2 rakats Fajr sunnah, nafil before Maghrib fard, nafil during Jumu'ah khutbah
- **Book structure:** Tahara → Timings → Adhan (logical flow of prerequisites)
- **Adhan is only for** the 5 daily prayers and Jumu'ah -- proven by Tawatur Amali
- **How Adhan was established:** Fire, bell, and horn were rejected; an angel taught the words in a dream
- **No tarji' in Hanafi adhan** -- Abu Mahzurah's practice was a misunderstanding of a teaching moment
- **الصَّلَاةُ خَيْرٌ مِنَ النَّوْمِ** is specific to Fajr adhan only, from the words of Bilal
- **In heavy rain:** announce صَلُّوا فِي رِحَالِكُمْ separately -- don't add it to the adhan
