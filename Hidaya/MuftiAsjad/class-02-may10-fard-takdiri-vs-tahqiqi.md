*A simplified summary of the lecture covering the hierarchy between theoretical and actual fard obligations, and how this affects what you can pray during certain times*

## The Core Concept: Fard Takdiri vs Fard Tahqiqi

This was the central discussion of today's class -- understanding two levels of fard and how they relate to each other.

### What is Fard Takdiri?

**Fard Takdiri** means: a fard prayer's **theoretical right** to occupy its entire time window. The person hasn't actually started praying it yet, but he **can** pray it at any point during that window. It's a potential -- it hasn't occurred yet, but it can occur.

For example: Dhuhr time starts at 12:30 and lasts for about 3 hours. Dhuhr salah has the **takdiri right** to be read at any moment from the beginning of that window to the end. From the first minute, from the middle, or right at the end -- its right extends across the entire time.

The **azimat** (most rewarding way) is to cover the whole time -- meaning to pray early and let the remaining time serve as a buffer. That's considered very great in sawab (reward).

### What is Fard Tahqiqi?

**Fard Tahqiqi** (also called Fard Haqiqi or Tahkiqi) means: a fard prayer that a person is **actually performing right now**. He is acting upon it. It's real, not theoretical.

For example: You are actually reading a qada fard prayer during Dhuhr time -- you are physically praying it. That's tahqiqi.

---

## The Hierarchy

This is the key takeaway:

> **Fard Tahqiqi > Fard Takdiri > Nafil**

In plain terms:

1. **Actually reading a fard prayer** (tahqiqi) is more superior than the theoretical right of another fard (takdiri)
2. **The theoretical right of a fard** (takdiri) is more superior than nawafil (voluntary prayers)
3. **Nafil** is the lowest in this hierarchy

### What This Means Practically

- **You CAN read qada fard prayers** during the time of another fard -- because fard tahqiqi beats fard takdiri
- **You CANNOT read nafil prayers** when there is a fard takdiri claim on that time -- because fard takdiri beats nafil
- This is the reason behind the rulings we've been learning about makruh prayer times

---

## Detailed Example: Dhuhr Time

Let's say Dhuhr time is from 12:30 to 3:30 (3 hours).

- Dhuhr salah has the **takdiri right** to be read at any point in this window -- beginning, middle, or end
- If someone hasn't read a previous fard (say he missed Isha from last night), is it better for him to read that qada fard or to read nafil in the remaining Dhuhr time?
- **Answer:** Reading the qada fard is better. Why? Because nafil salah will not be asked about on the Day of Judgement, but fard salah **will** be asked about. So which one has more right to that time? The fard.
- The qada fard he is actually praying (tahqiqi) is more superior than the Dhuhr fard's theoretical claim (takdiri) on that remaining time

---

## Detailed Example: Fajr Time

The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: **do not read any salah apart from the two rakats of Fajr sunnah after subh sadiq until sunrise.**

But you **can** read qada fard prayers in that time. Why? The same principle:

- After you finish the 2 rakats of sunnah (in about 10 minutes), let's say there are 30 minutes remaining until the Fajr iqamah
- That remaining 30 minutes belongs to Fajr fard -- that's the **fard takdiri**
- If you read nafil, nafil is NOT more superior than fard takdiri -- so you can't read nafil
- But if you actually read another fard salah (qada), that's **fard tahqiqi** -- which IS more superior than fard takdiri -- so you can

## What About Fawait (Missed Prayers)?

Fawait are fard prayers you missed and are now making qada of. When you read fawait:

- You are **actually acting upon it** -- you are reading that fard salah
- This is **tahqiqi** -- real, happening right now
- The fard of the current time (say Dhuhr) is **takdiri** -- it could have been prolonged to fill the time, but you chose not to
- Since tahqiqi beats takdiri, you can read your fawait in that time

## Practical Scenario: Missed Isha and Now It's Fajr

The teacher walked through a real-life scenario:

**Situation:** A person didn't read Isha last night. Now it's Fajr time.

**If he is Sahib al-Tartib** (someone who maintains the order of prayers and has fewer than 6 missed): he **must** read Isha first, then Fajr. The order is mandatory for him.

**If he has already read Fajr:** The first thing he must do after that is make qada of Isha. That wujub (obligation) is on his head. If he were to die, he would be questioned about it. He needs to remove that **zimmadari** (responsibility) from himself.

**The better approach:** It is better for him to read Fajr **short** (quickly, with minimum requirements) and then read Isha **short** as well -- fitting both in before the time ends -- rather than reading Fajr luxuriously for the entire time window and not getting to Isha at all.

Why? Because fitting in both fard prayers (even if short) is better than perfecting one and missing the other.

## Q&A From the Class

### Q: After reading Dhuhr fard, should I read qada before sunnah?

**A student asked:** If someone has a qada obligation and he just finished Dhuhr fard, should he read the qada before reading the Dhuhr sunnah?

**Answer:** Yes, he should. The qada fard takes priority over sunnah.

---

### Q: Is there a naqis (deficient) time for Asr?

**A student asked** about the relationship between the Fajr/sunrise ruling and the Asr/sunset ruling -- specifically whether Asr has a naqis (deficient) time.

**Answer:** For Asr, there is **no naqis time**. The entire Asr time -- from start all the way to ghurub -- is the same. The karahah (dislike) doesn't create a separate "deficient window" the way it does with Fajr and sunrise. It's all one continuous time for Asr.

---

### Q: Can you read Tahiyyat al-Masjid after Fajr sunnah at home?

**A student asked:** If a person reads his Fajr sunnah at home, then goes to the masjid and there is still time before the iqamah -- should he read Tahiyyat al-Masjid (the prayer of greeting the masjid)?

**Answer:** **No, you cannot.** It would be **makruh**. If you can't read nafil salah in those timings (after Fajr sunnah until sunrise), then Tahiyyat al-Masjid is also nafil -- so the same ruling applies. You just sit and wait for the iqamah.

**The Shafi'i view:** They say you **must** do Tahiyyat al-Masjid even in those times because according to them Tahiyyat al-Masjid is **wajib**. So they don't consider it regular nafil -- they see it as an obligation triggered by entering the masjid.

---

## Quick Summary

- **Fard Takdiri** = the theoretical right of a fard prayer to fill its entire time window (potential, not yet acted on)
- **Fard Tahqiqi** = actually performing a fard prayer right now (real, happening)
- **Hierarchy:** Fard Tahqiqi > Fard Takdiri > Nafil
- **That's why** qada fard can be read during another fard's time -- tahqiqi beats takdiri
- **That's why** nafil can't be read when fard takdiri has a claim -- takdiri beats nafil
- **Missed Isha at Fajr time:** If Sahib al-Tartib, read Isha first then Fajr. Otherwise, read both short rather than one long
- **Qada before sunnah:** Yes, qada fard takes priority over sunnah after the fard
- **No Tahiyyat al-Masjid** after Fajr sunnah in the Hanafi view -- it's nafil and the time is claimed by fard takdiri
- **Shafi'is** allow Tahiyyat al-Masjid because they consider it wajib
